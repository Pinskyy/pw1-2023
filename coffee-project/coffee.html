Our History

An idea came when one time we received a shipment of Sumatran coffee that he felt was very sticky and hard. This Lampung specialty coffee was sent by a colleague from school. Because we felt it was too strong, we mixed this Lampung coffee with regular Java coffee that we could easily find from ground coffee vendors in our city.

At the same time, we had a discussion with a ground coffee vendor in our town about the flavor effects of using firewood in the coffee roasting process compared to roasting in an oven or roasting on a gas stove.

From there, the idea came to us to make our own coffee concoction by trying out ideas that came to mind. So we tried to mix Lampung specialty coffee with Javanese coffee which before grinding, roasted with firewood made of teak wood.

We served this concoction to several colleagues and admittedly this unique blend produced a more alluring taste on the tongue. It is not sticky like coffee from Sumatra but also has a fresh, sanguine aroma from the teak wood roasting process. This was the beginning when we finally saw that this could be a great idea for a new business. And so was born the idea of a coffee shop business that we have now been running for 4 years.
